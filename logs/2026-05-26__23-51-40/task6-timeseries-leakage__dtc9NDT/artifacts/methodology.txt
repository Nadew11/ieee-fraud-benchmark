# Methodology Note: Fraud Detection with Engineered Features

## 1. Feature Engineering Approach
To build a high-performing fraud classifier while strictly preventing future data leakage, we engineered several historical and velocity-based features. Crucially, all historical aggregation calculations used shift() operations so that transaction data from the present moment is not used to calculate historical statistics (avoiding lookahead bias).

Key engineered features include:
- **Time Velocity Features**: `dt_diff_card1` and `dt_diff_card1_addr1` measure the elapsed time since the previous transaction for a given card/region combo, helping flag rapid-fire bursts of suspicious activity.
- **Cumulative Transaction Counts**: Track the evolving frequency of a cardholder's transactions over the 6-month period.
- **Cumulative Transaction Amount Statistics**: Cumulative mean, standard deviation, and relative transaction amount ratio (e.g., current transaction amount vs historical mean transaction amount for the card).
- **Cumulative Fraud Rates (Target Encoding)**: Running historical ratio of fraud for a given card or cardholder location, using a shift operator to ensure no target leakage.
- **Cyclical Time Feature**: Estimating the hour of the day from the elapsed seconds to capture temporal patterns in fraud activity.

## 2. Evaluation Strategy
We used a strict chronological/temporal split to mirror production deployment:
- **Training set**: The first 80% of chronological transactions (spanning approximately the first 145 days).
- **Test set**: The final 20% of chronological transactions (representing the subsequent ~37 days of future transactions).

An XGBoost classifier with regularized depth, column subsampling, and low learning rate was trained on the training split and evaluated on the test set.
