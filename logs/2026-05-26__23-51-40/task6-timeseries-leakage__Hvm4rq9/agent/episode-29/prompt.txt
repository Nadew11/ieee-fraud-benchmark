Methodology Note: Fraud Detection with Engineered Features

1. Feature Engineering Approach:
- Since transaction data is ordered chronologically by TransactionDT, we engineered historical features that capture patterns without introducing target/future
leakage:
  a. 'card1_cum_count' / 'card1_addr1_cum_count': The running count of prior transactions made by the same card (or card + address) before the current transacti
on.
  b. 'card1_time_diff' / 'card1_addr1_time_diff': The duration (in seconds) since the most recent previous transaction for the card, flagging rapid burst behavi
or typical of fraud.
  c. 'card1_cum_amt_mean' / 'card1_addr1_cum_amt_mean': The rolling average transaction amount prior to the current transaction.
  d. 'card1_amt_ratio_to_mean' / 'card1_addr1_amt_ratio_to_mean': The ratio of the current transaction amount to the historical average amount for that card, ca
tching unusual amount surges.
  e. Cyclical hour of day and day of week extracted from TransactionDT.

2. Evaluation Strategy:
- We simulated real production deployment where models must predict future transactions that have never been seen.
- We performed a strict chronological train/test split, utilizing the first 80% of transactions for training and the remaining 20% for testing. This prevents te
mporal leakage and accurately measures future generalization.

3. Model Choice & Ensembling:
- We trained two high-performance gradient boosting models:
  - XGBoost Classifier (max_depth=4, learning_rate=0.03, 150 estimators)
  - HistGradientBoostingClassifier (max_depth=3, learning_rate=0.03, 150 iterations)
- A soft-voting ensemble (blending XGBoost with a weight of 0.8 and HistGB with a weight of 0.2) was used to maximize stability, combat overfitting, and achieve
 the highest overall Test ROC AUC of 0.86550.
