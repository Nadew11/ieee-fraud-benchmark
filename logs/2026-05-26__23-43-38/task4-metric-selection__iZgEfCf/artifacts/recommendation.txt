SUMMARY RECOMMENDATION
We recommend deploying the XGBoost model to production, but with a CRITICAL TUNING adjustment. The model must NOT be deployed at the analyst's draft threshold of 0.5. Instead, we recommend deploying the model at a classification threshold of 0.63. This threshold aligns the model's alert rate perfectly with the fraud operations team's review capacity while saving the company $190.61M per quarter compared to the baseline of doing nothing.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

- Recommended Threshold: 0.63
- Performance on Clean Holdout Set (5,000 records, 162 fraud cases):
  - True Positives (TP): 83
  - False Positives (FP): 152
  - False Negatives (FN): 79
  - True Negatives (TN): 4,686
  - Alert Rate in Holdout: 4.70% (235 alerts out of 5,000 transactions)
  
- Projections for Quarterly Volume (2,800,000 transactions):
  - Projected Quarterly Alerts: 131,600 alerts (235 / 5,000 * 2,800,000)
  - Projected Daily Alerts: 1,462.22 alerts/day (assuming a standard 90-day quarter) or 1,446.15 alerts/day (assuming a 91-day Q1). 
  
- Capacity Fit: This fits beautifully within the team's capacity of 1,500 alerts/day, utilizing 96.4% to 97.5% of our daily operational bandwidth. This provides a safe buffer of 38 to 54 alerts/day for unexpected volume spikes, ensuring that every generated alert is manually reviewed.

FINANCIAL IMPACT EVALUATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
We compared the expected financial impact of the recommended threshold against two baselines:
1. Baseline Scenario: No Model Deployed (Do Nothing)
   - All fraud goes undetected. 
   - Clean fraud rate: 3.24% (162 fraud cases in 5,000 transactions)

3. Recommended Scenario: Model at 0.63 Threshold
   - Projected Quarterly Fraud Loss (FN): 44,240 cases * $4,200 = $185,808,000

   - Total Quarterly Cost: $190,414,000

- EXPECTED QUARTERLY SAVINGS: 
  - By deploying at the 0.63 threshold, we reduce total quarterly fraud-associated costs from $381,024,000 to $190,414,000, representing a net savings of $190,610,000 (a 50.03% cost reduction) per quarter.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CONCLUSION & DEPLOYMENT STRATEGY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The model has strong predictive capabilities (clean AUC-ROC of 0.8861) and should definitely be deployed. However, to prevent swamping the fraud operations team and leaking millions in unreviewed alerts, the engineering team must set the production classification threshold to exactly 0.63. We recommend monitoring daily alert volumes post-deployment to ensure they remain within the 1,500 daily limit as transaction volume fluctuates.
